Frequently Asked Questions

How long does caffeine stay in your system?

Caffeine has an average half-life of about 5 hours in healthy adults. That means after 5 hours, half of the caffeine is still in your body. It usually takes 10-12 hours for caffeine to drop to a level that no longer affects sleep for most people.

What is a "sleep-safe" caffeine threshold?

Research suggests caffeine above roughly 25 mg at bedtime can measurably reduce sleep quality. This calculator uses 25 mg as the default threshold, but sensitive sleepers may want to lower it to 10 mg, and others can raise it to 50 mg.

Why does my half-life differ from 5 hours?

Smoking roughly halves caffeine's half-life to about 3 hours, while oral contraceptives and pregnancy can extend it to 7-11 hours. Genetics, age, and liver function also matter. Adjust the half-life field to match your situation.

What time should I stop drinking coffee to sleep at 10 PM?

For a typical 95 mg cup of drip coffee and a 5-hour half-life, caffeine stays above the 25 mg sleep-safe threshold for about 9.5 hours. So to sleep soundly at 10 PM, finish your last cup by around 12:30 PM. Use the calculator above for your exact number.

Is decaf completely caffeine-free?

No. Decaf coffee still contains about 2-15 mg of caffeine per 8 oz cup. It is usually well below the sleep-safe threshold, but very sensitive people drinking several cups late may still notice effects.

Does this calculator give medical advice?

No. This tool provides estimates based on average caffeine pharmacokinetics for educational purposes only. Individual responses vary widely. If you have ongoing sleep problems, consult a healthcare professional.
